Introduction
What is Perf Lab and what can it do?

Welcome to TurboFPS Perf Lab — your all-in-one performance optimization hub for Android. Perf Lab gives you direct control over your device's CPU, GPU, and system-level resources so you can squeeze every drop of performance out of your phone, especially during gaming sessions.

This guide walks you through every feature in Perf Lab — what it does, how to use it, and important safety notices you should read before enabling experimental features.

Before You Begin
Perf Lab requires Shizuku to be active before any feature can work. Shizuku is a free tool that grants TurboFPS the permissions needed to apply system-level optimizations without root. Some features also require a TurboFPS Premium license — free users can unlock these by watching a short rewarded ad.

CPU Optimization
Processor performance control

The CPU Optimization section lets you control how your processor performs — whether you want maximum power for gaming or a balanced profile for everyday use.

3.1 CPU Boost

CPU Boost activates a high-performance mode for your processor, prioritizing speed and responsiveness over battery saving. The CPU Governor is pushed toward performance, resulting in smoother gameplay, faster app launches, and reduced input lag.

  • 1
    Open Perf Lab and locate the CPU Optimization section.
  • 2
    Toggle the CPU Boost switch to ON.
  • 3
    If prompted, watch the short rewarded ad (free users) or it activates instantly (licensed users).
  • 4
    The status label will update to Active when the boost is successfully applied.
Warning
Enabling CPU Boost will increase battery consumption and device temperature. Do not leave CPU Boost enabled for extended periods when the device is idle. If your device becomes unusually hot, disable CPU Boost immediately.
3.2 Manual Mode — CPU Governor

Manual Mode gives you precise control over the CPU Governor — the algorithm that decides how your CPU scales its performance in real time.

  • 1
    Toggle the Manual Mode switch under the CPU section.
  • 2
    A governor selection card will expand below.
  • 3
    Pick your preferred governor from the available options.
  • 4
    Tap Apply to save. TurboFPS will remember your choice next time.

Available CPU Governors:

PowerSave
Keeps CPU frequency low. Best for battery saving during light tasks.
Balanced
Default mode. Adjusts CPU speed based on current workload.
Schedutil
Responsive scaling based on CPU scheduler feedback. Good all-rounder for gaming.
Performance
Locks CPU at maximum frequency. Best for gaming but drains battery fastest.

GPU Optimization
Graphics processor performance control

The GPU Optimization section provides controls for your device's graphics processor — directly impacting frame rates, visual smoothness, and render quality in games.

4.1 GPU Boost

GPU Boost activates a performance-oriented mode for your graphics processor, helping deliver higher and more stable frame rates in demanding games like BGMI, Call of Duty Mobile, and Blood Strike.

  • 1
    Locate the GPU Optimization section in Perf Lab.
  • 2
    Toggle the GPU Boost switch to ON.
  • 3
    Watch the rewarded ad if prompted (free users), or it activates instantly (licensed users).
  • 4
    Status label will show Active upon success.
Warning
GPU Boost significantly increases GPU load and heat output. Extended use may cause thermal throttling on devices with limited cooling. Monitor your device temperature during long gaming sessions.
4.2 Manual Mode — GPU Governor

Manual Mode for GPU lets you select the rendering governor that controls how your GPU scales its clock speeds.

  • 1
    Toggle the Manual Mode switch in the GPU section.
  • 2
    Select your preferred GPU governor from the expanded card.
  • 3
    Tap Apply to save. Your selection persists across app restarts.
PowerSave
Reduces GPU frequency for lower power consumption and heat.
Fast
Quickly ramps up GPU frequency when load increases. Good for gaming.
Adaptive
Dynamically adjusts GPU speed based on real-time render demands.
Performance
Maximizes GPU frequency. Best frame rates but highest battery drain.

Per-Game Booster
Targeted performance boost for a specific app

Per-Game Booster lets you apply a targeted performance boost to a specific app on your device for a set duration. Instead of globally boosting your entire device, TurboFPS focuses system resources on the game you choose — resulting in better frame stability and lower input latency.

Availability Note
Per-Game Booster is not available on iQOO, Vivo, Samsung, and Itel devices due to manufacturer-level restrictions on system resource management.
5.1 How to Use
  • 1
    Tap Add Apps and select the game or app you want to boost.
  • 2
    Choose a Boost Level: Performance, Extreme, or Ultra.
  • 3
    Choose a Boost Duration: 30 minutes, 1 hour, or 2 hours.
  • 4
    Make sure the selected game is already running in the foreground or background.
  • 5
    Tap Apply Boost. A countdown timer will appear showing the remaining boost time. The boost automatically deactivates when the timer ends.
5.2 Boost Levels
PERFORMANCE
Performance
Moderate boost. Improves frame stability without aggressive power usage. Suitable for everyday gaming.
EXTREME
Extreme
Strong boost. Prioritizes the selected app with more CPU/GPU headroom. Good for demanding titles.
ULTRA
Ultra
Maximum boost. All available resources focused on the selected app. Use for short, competitive sessions.
5.3 Boost Durations
DurationBest for
30 MinutesQuick session. Ideal for casual matches or short gaming bursts.
1 HourStandard gaming session. Good balance between performance and battery life.
2 HoursExtended session. Use for long grinding sessions. Monitor device temperature.
Warning
The target app must be running before you tap Apply Boost, otherwise the boost will not activate. Ultra mode for 2 hours will cause significant battery drain and heating. Per-Game Boost state is saved — if you close TurboFPS and reopen it, the countdown resumes automatically.

PowerHints Manager
Advanced Optimization — power users only

PowerHints Manager gives you granular control over system-level performance hints. These hints signal the device's power management layer to prioritize specific hardware subsystems, resulting in smoother performance for specific use cases.

Warning — Advanced Optimization
These features are experimental and intended for power users only. You must first enable Unlock Advanced Mode. PowerHints may cause overheating, increased battery drain, or unexpected device shutdowns. Known issues reported on Samsung and Vivo devices. Proceed with caution. Use entirely at your own risk.
6.1 Enabling Advanced Mode
  • 1
    Scroll to the Advanced Optimization section in Perf Lab.
  • 2
    Toggle Unlock Advanced Mode to ON.
  • 3
    All experimental features and controls will become visible.
  • 4
    Toggling it back OFF will hide all experimental features and reset the Custom Delay toggle.
6.2 Using PowerHints Manager
  • 1
    Tap the PowerHints Manager card to expand it.
  • 2
    Toggle Enable Custom Hints to ON. (Requires Shizuku.)
  • 3
    Select one or more hint chips from the list below.
  • 4
    Tap Apply to activate your selected hints.
6.3 Available Power Hints

Experimental Features
Deep system-level optimizations — read carefully

These features appear in the Experimental section once Advanced Mode is unlocked. They apply deep system-level optimizations that go beyond standard performance tuning. Behavior differs depending on your device brand.

Warning — Experimental Features
All Experimental features require Advanced Mode to be ON. Not available on iQOO, Vivo, Samsung, and Itel devices (except Multi Renderer and Render Engine Boost, which have adjusted behavior on those brands). Do not combine multiple experimental features without understanding the impact. If any feature causes instability, toggle it off immediately.
Multi Renderer
Enables multithreaded rendering, allowing the GPU to process multiple render threads simultaneously. Can improve frame rates in complex scenes. Behavior is adjusted on Samsung/Vivo/iQOO.
All Brands
Render Engine Boost
Applies deep GPU pipeline optimizations via the system's surface rendering engine. Can reduce frame jitter. Behavior varies by device brand.
All Brands
Game Manager
Activates the system-level game manager to improve resource scheduling during gaming.
Restricted
Sensor Acceleration
Fine-tunes sensor polling and processing to deliver sharper and faster in-game feedback. Behavior varies by brand.
Restricted
No Limits Mode
Removes all battery optimization restrictions from the system. The device will not throttle performance due to battery policies.
Restricted
Predictive Rendering
Enables the system to predict and pre-render upcoming frames based on motion data, reducing perceived latency.
Restricted
Tip
Enable one experimental feature at a time, test it in your game for a few minutes, then add another if needed. This helps you identify which feature improves performance on your specific device.

Customize Sensors Delay
Manual sensor reporting interval control

The Customize Sensors Delay panel lets you manually set the reporting delay for specific hardware sensors on your device. Reducing sensor delay means the game receives more frequent and precise input data — especially useful for gyroscope-heavy FPS games.

Availability
This feature is only available when Advanced Mode is ON. Not available on iQOO, Vivo, Samsung, and Itel devices.
8.1 How to Use
  • 1
    Unlock Advanced Mode (see Section 6.1).
  • 2
    Toggle the Customize Sensors Delay button at the bottom of the Advanced section.
  • 3
    Select a sensor from the sensor selector (e.g., Accelerometer, Gyroscope, etc.).
  • 4
    Adjust the delay slider. Range is 0 ms to 50 ms in steps of 5 ms.
  • 5
    Tap Apply to set the delay for the selected sensor, or apply it globally to all sensors.
8.2 Delay Guide
5 – 10 ms
Best for Gaming
Sharpest and most responsive input. Slightly higher battery usage.
15 – 20 ms
Good Balance
Good balance between responsiveness and battery. Suitable for casual games.
25 ms
Default
Standard system delay. Normal battery usage.
30 – 50 ms
Relaxed
Lower power consumption. Not recommended for competitive gaming.
Warning
Setting delay too low (0–5 ms) on older devices may cause frame drops or increased CPU usage. Always test after applying a new delay setting to confirm stability in your game.

Troubleshooting
Common issues and how to fix them
Feature doesn't activate / switch reverts
Shizuku is not running. Open Shizuku, activate it, then try again.
CPU/GPU Boost shows "Inactive"
Check that Shizuku is active. Ensure you have a valid license or watch the rewarded ad.
Per-Game Booster won't apply
Make sure the target app is already running. Select a boost level and duration before tapping Apply.
Experimental features are hidden
Scroll to Advanced Optimization and enable Unlock Advanced Mode.
Feature not available on my device
iQOO, Vivo, Samsung, and Itel devices have restrictions on certain features. This is expected behavior.
Device overheating after enabling features
Disable CPU Boost, GPU Boost, and all Experimental features. Allow the device to cool down.
App crashes after enabling a power hint
Some hints may be incompatible with your device. Disable all hints and re-enable one at a time to isolate the issue.
Sensor delay change causes frame drops
Increase the delay value. Setting below 10 ms may be too aggressive for older hardware.

Safety & Best Practices
Use Perf Lab responsibly
Start Simple
Always start with standard CPU/GPU Boost before exploring Advanced or Experimental features.
Test Incrementally
Enable one feature at a time and test your game before adding more.
Disable When Idle

Monitor Temperature
Keep a thermometer app or check device temperature regularly during extended boost sessions.
Know Your Risk
Advanced Mode and Experimental features are intended for experienced users who understand the trade-offs.
Keep Shizuku Updated
Always keep Shizuku updated to ensure compatibility with the latest Android security patches.
